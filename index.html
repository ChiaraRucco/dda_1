Functional Areas — Responsibilities & Interfaces
Data Architecture
Blueprints the platform and data models to enable scale, resiliency, and reuse.
  • Owns target architectures (Lakehouse/Medallion), integration patterns, and data contracts.
  • Chairs the Architecture Review for projects and approves cross-domain designs.
  • Defines canonical models, reference/master entity relationships, and interoperability rules.
  • Sets standards for security, cost, SLAs, and observability across pillars.
Interfaces with: Data Eng (pipelines), BI (semantic models), AI (features), Governance (policies/MDM).
Data Management
Builds reliable pipelines and consumable analytics layers.
  • Data Engineering: ingestion, ELT/ETL, orchestration, CI/CD pipeline automation, quality gates, performance & cost optimization.
  • BI Engineering: curated marts, semantic models, KPI frameworks, enterprise datasets.
  • Data Visualization: UX, storytelling, dashboard standards, accessibility, and adoption.
Interfaces with: Architecture (patterns/contracts), AI (features/serving data), Business stakeholders (consumption).
AI
Delivers ML/AI solutions and keeps models healthy in production.
  • ML Eng & Data Science: problem framing, experimentation, model training, evaluation and responsible AI checks.
  • MLOps: model deployment, CI/CD for ML, monitoring (drift, bias, performance), rollback and governance.
  • Partners with Data Eng for feature pipelines and with BI for human-in-the-loop decisioning.
Interfaces with: Architecture (platform/standards), Data Management (features/data), Governance (policy & AI compliance).
Data Governance
Makes data trustworthy and compliant while enabling access.
  • Owns policies, access control models, DPIA/PIA processes, and regulatory alignment (e.g., GDPR, AI Act).
  • Drives data ownership, stewardship operating model, and issue remediation workflows.
  • Owns MDM: golden records, match/merge rules, reference data authoring and lifecycle.
  • Publishes glossary, rules of engagement, and certification criteria for data products.
Interfaces with: All pillars to embed compliance and quality-by-design.
Project Distribution Model — Rationale & Decision Rules

Tech Lead assignment is based on scope, cross-domain impact, architectural novelty, and risk. Use the rules below to ensure safety and speed.

Large / Multidisciplinary Project High Impact
Tech Lead → Data Architecture (Lead/Senior Data Solutions Architect)
Why this assignment?
  • Cross-pillar coupling (Data, BI, AI, Governance) requires end-to-end architectural oversight.
  • Introduces or modifies reusable assets (data models, contracts, platform capabilities) that affect multiple teams.
  • Non-functional requirements (SLA, scale, security, cost) must be balanced across domains.
  • Minimizes rework by enforcing standards and patterns upfront.
Medium Project – Single Discipline Scoped
Tech Lead → Corresponding Lead role (Lead Data Engineer, Lead BI Engineer, Lead MLOps Engineer, Lead ML Engineer).
Why this assignment?
  • Work is contained within one pillar; dependencies are known and limited.
  • Enables faster iteration while still applying pillar standards and checklists.
  • Architecture is not materially changed (uses established patterns).
Small / Niche Project Operational
Tech Lead → Data Visualization Specialist or BI Engineer (if reporting-only or minor data shaping).
Why this assignment?
  • Scope is localized to consumption layer; minimal platform or data model impact.
  • Optimizes time-to-value by keeping ownership with the delivery team closest to the user.
  • Complexity/risks can be managed with standard datasets and design system.
